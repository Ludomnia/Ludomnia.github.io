Let's make a Character!

In this page we will show you a different (and possibly more fun) way to make a character, using as a base the Cypher System Rulebook:

  1. The stats: every character starts with 34 stats point to divide among Strenght, Speed and Intellect, with no limits regarding the distribution except for the cap at 20 points.
  2. Edge: every character gets one edge point at the start to place in the stat they prefer.
    Attention! If you also accept the burden of a weakness in a stat (a -1 edge in a stat) you can get an additional point of edge where you prefer.
  3. Effort: all characters start with one level of effort
  4. Special Abilities: the special abilities in our system work based of slots: a high tier will need 3 slots, a mid tier 2 slot and a low tier 1 slot. At tier 1 you start with 4 slots that you can manage however you want. For example you could buy one high tier and one low tier, or two mid tiers.
  5. Cyphers: a character can carry as many as 3 cyphers at one time
    Attention! Choosing to give up one or even two slots for cyphers will reward you with 1 or 2 more slots for your special abilities.
  6. Weapons: a character can use all kind of weapons or decide to give up certain kinds to get additional special abilities slot. For every kind you give up you'll get 1 additional special ability slot. To be able to use that weapon kind again you must pay a penalty.
  7. Stats Bonuses: character can have a +4 or a +2 in evry stats but this will influence the amount of slot for the skills (yes, skills too use a slot system).
  8. Skills: as said before, skills too have a slot based system, that can be used to buy narrow or broad skills. Narrow skills cost only one slot while the broad skills (being a collection of multiple narrow skills) cost 2 slots. Getting a +2 in the stats will reward you 2 skill slots, while getting the +4 grants 2 slots.
  9. Inability: if you decide to accept an inability in a skill you'll be granted another skill slot. This "conversion" can only be done twice, granting a maximum of two extra slots
